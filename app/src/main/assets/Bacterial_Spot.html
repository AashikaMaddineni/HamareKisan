Caused By:

Bacteria (Xanthomonas spp. & Pseudomonas syringae pv. Tomato).


Stages:

Vegetative state, Flowering stage, Fruiting stage, Harvesting stage


Symptoms:

Small dark spots with a yellow halo on leaves and fruits. Stem and flower stalk also affected.


Organic Control:

Bacterial Spot is very expensive to treat. If disease occurs early in the season consider destroying the entire crop. Copper containing bactericides provide a protective cover on foliage and fruit for both bacteria. Bacterial viruses that specifically kill the bacteria are available for bacterial spot. Submerging seeds for one minute in 1.3% sodium hypochlorite or in hot water(50o C) for 25 minutes can reduce the incidence of disease


Chemical Control

  1. Copper Oxychloride 50.0% WP (Moderately toxic)

    Instructions:

    Apply product 7 times with 7 days gap. Spray. Don't apply if you are harvesting in the next 14 days or if it is windy or raining. For 1 Acre, use 1 Kilogram. Dilute in 354 Litre of water.


  2. Mancozeb 75.0% WP (Slightly toxic)

    Instructions:

    Apply product 2 times with 15 days gap. Spray. Don't apply if it is windy or raining. For 1 Acre, use 405 gram. Dilute in 202 Litre of water.


  3. Streptomycin sulfate 90.0% SP, Tetracycline hydrochloride 10.0% SP (Highly toxic)

    Instructions:

    Apply product 2 times with 11 days gap. Spray. Don't apply if it is windy or raining. For 1 Acre, use 36 Gram. Dilute in 182 Litre of water.
