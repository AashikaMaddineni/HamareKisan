Spider mites Two-spotted spider mite

Caused By:

Mite (Tetranychidae).


Stages:

Vegetative state, Flowering stage, Fruiting stage.


Symptoms:

Tiny spots on leaves. Small webs can be found between stem. Dried out leaves. Tiny, pale green, oval mites.


Organic Control:

In case of minor infestation, simply wash off the mites and remove the affected leaves. Use preparations based on rapeseed, basil, soybean and neem oils to spray leaves thoroughly and reduce populations of T. urticae. Also try garlic tea, nettle slurry or insecticidal soap solutions to control the population. In fields, employ host-specific biological control with predatory mites (for example Phytoseiulus persimilis) or the biological pesticide Bacillus thuringiensis. A second spray treatment application 2 to 3 days after the initial treatment is necessary.


Chemical Control

  1. Spiromesifen 22.9%SC (Slightly toxic)

    Instructions:

    Apply product only once. Spray. Don’t apply if you are harvesting in the next 3 days or if it is windy or raining. For 1 Acre, use 253 millilitre . Dilute in 202 Litre of water.


  2. Fenazaquin 10.0% BC (Moderately toxic)

    Instructions:

    Apply product only once. Spray. Don’t apply if you are harvesting in the next 5 days or if it is windy or raining. For 1 Acre, use 1 Kilogram. Dilute in 506 Litre of water.


  3. Abamectin 1.8% EC (Highly toxic)

    Instructions:

    Apply product only once. Spray. Don’t apply if you are harvesting in the next 14 days or if it is windy or raining. For 1 Acre, use 101 millilitre . Dilute in 202 Litre of water.
