Late Blight

Caused By:

Fungus (Phytophthora infestans)


Stages:

Fruiting stage, Harvesting stage


Symptoms:

Brown leaf spots - start from margin. White covering on the underside of leaves. Grey or brown wrinkled stains on fruits. Hardened fruit flesh spots and fruit decay.


Organic Control:

At this point, there is no biological control of known efficacy against late blight. To avoid spreading, remove and destroy plants around the infected spot immediately and do not compost infected plant material.


Chemical Control

  1. Cymoxanil 22.1% SC, Famoxadone 16.6% SC (Moderately toxic)

    Instructions:

    Apply product 5 times with 10 days gap. Spray. Don’t apply if you are harvesting in the next 3 days or if it is windy or raining. For 1 Acre, use 202 Millilitre. Dilute in 202 Litre of water.


  2. Chlorothalonil 33.1% SC, Metalaxyl-M 3.3% SC (Moderately toxic)

    Instructions:

    Apply product 3 times with 15 days gap. Spray. Don’t apply if you are harvesting in the next 5 days or if it is windy or raining. For 1 Acre, use 405 Millilitre. Dilute in 202 Litre of water.


  3. Mandipropamid 23.4% SC (Slightly toxic)

    Instructions:

    Apply product 2 times with 8 days gap. Spray. Don’t apply if you are harvesting in the next 5 days or if it is windy or raining. For 1 Acre, use 162 Millilitre. Dilute in 202 Litre of water.

  4. Mancozeb 75.0% WP (Slightly toxic)

    Instructions:

    Apply product 2 times with 15 days gap. Spray. Don’t apply if you are harvesting in the next 5 days or if it is windy or raining. For 1 Acre, use 405 Gram. Dilute in 202 Litre of water.
